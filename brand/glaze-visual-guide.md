# Glaze Visual Guide

> Visual characteristics for studio glazes to improve AI detection accuracy.

## Detection Philosophy

**CRITICAL RULE: Surface Evidence → Glaze Identification**

Look at surface qualities FIRST, then match to likely glaze:
1. **Texture** (smooth vs textured) eliminates entire glaze families
2. **Sheen** (matte/satin/gloss) narrows options
3. **Color + Movement** identifies specific glazes

---

## The #1 Confusion: Teadust vs Shino

| Surface Evidence | Indicates |
|------------------|-----------|
| **SMOOTH + uniform speckle** | TEADUST |
| **TEXTURED** (crackle, crawling, variegation) | SHINO |

**Never** call something Teadust if you see crackle, crawling, or texture variation.

## Shino Family

### Malcom's Shino
- **Color**: Orange-tan to reddish-brown
- **Sheen**: Satin to semi-gloss
- **Surface**: TEXTURED - often has crawling, crackle
- **Movement**: Variable, can have rivulets
- **Clay bodies**: Works well on B-Mix, Death Valley
- **Layering**: Often paired with Luster Shino

### Luster Shino
- **Color**: Bronze-gold metallic sheen over orange-brown
- **Sheen**: Satin with metallic luster
- **Surface**: TEXTURED - crawling common
- **Movement**: Metallic pools in recesses
- **Use**: Almost always layered OVER another glaze for luster effect

**Shino Combo**: "Malcom's Shino + Luster Shino" is a common layered combination

---

## Clear Glazes

### Lucid Clear
- **Color**: Transparent, shows clay body
- **Sheen**: High gloss
- **Surface**: Smooth
- **Movement**: Pools in recesses (darker where thick)
- **Use**: Over colored slips or textured surfaces

### Tom Coleman Clear
- **Color**: Transparent with slight amber tint when thick
- **Sheen**: High gloss
- **Surface**: Smooth
- **Movement**: Excellent pooling, shows texture
- **Layering**: Often top layer over colored glazes

## Green Family

### Froggy
- **Color**: Speckled green (varied tones)
- **Sheen**: Gloss
- **Surface**: Medium variegation, not smooth
- **Movement**: Color varies across surface
- **Look for**: Speckles and tonal variation

### Toady
- **Color**: Deeper green with possible brown tones
- **Sheen**: Gloss to semi-gloss
- **Surface**: Can have crawling/texture
- **Similar to**: Froggy but darker

### Ming Green
- **Color**: Uniform celadon green (translucent)
- **Sheen**: Gloss
- **Surface**: SMOOTH - no texture or crawling
- **Movement**: Pools in recesses (darker green)
- **Key**: Uniform, smooth, celadon-style

### Celadon
- **Color**: Light green, translucent
- **Sheen**: Gloss
- **Surface**: Smooth, glassy
- **Movement**: Color pools and varies with thickness
- **Key**: Traditional celadon look

### Amber Celadon
- **Color**: Green with amber/golden undertones
- **Sheen**: Gloss
- **Surface**: Smooth
- **Movement**: Amber shows in thin areas

## Layering Patterns (Common Combinations)

| Combination | Visual Result |
|-------------|---------------|
| Malcom's Shino + Luster Shino | Textured orange-brown with gold metallic sheen |
| Clear over Jensen Blue | Deep blue with glossy surface, pooling |
| Honey Luster over any glaze | Adds gold metallic accent over base color |
| White Crawl over colored slip | White crawling texture with color showing through |

## Detection Checklist

When analyzing a glazed piece:

1. **Is the surface SMOOTH or TEXTURED?**
   - Smooth → Eliminates Shinos, White Crawl
   - Textured → Likely Shino family or specialty crawling glaze

2. **What is the SHEEN level?**
   - Matte → Some specialty glazes, under-fired
   - Satin → Shinos, some celadons
   - Gloss → Clears, most colored glazes

3. **Is there METALLIC LUSTER?**
   - Yes → Honey Luster or Luster Shino involved

4. **Are there CRYSTALS?**
   - Yes → Strontium Crystal

5. **Is there COLOR POOLING?**
   - Yes → Transparent/translucent glaze (Clear, Celadon, Chun Blue)
