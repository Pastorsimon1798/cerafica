## Pricing Formula Analysis

### Standard Ceramic Pricing Formula

```
Materials + Labor + Overhead = Cost
Cost × 2 = Wholesale Price
Wholesale × 2 = Retail Price
```

### Applied to Your Work

**Materials:**
- Clay: $3-5 per piece
- Glaze materials (wood ash, iron oxide, volcanic ash): $8-12 per piece
- Firing costs (included in studio membership)
- **Total Materials: $11-17**

**Labor:**
- Throwing: 30-45 minutes
- Trimming: 15-20 minutes
- Glaze mixing (raw materials): 20-30 minutes
- Glaze application: 15-20 minutes
- Cleanup/studio time: 15 minutes
- **Total Labor: 1.5-2 hours = $45-60** (at $30/hour)

**Overhead:**
- Studio membership: $175-298/month
- If producing 20 pieces/month: $9-15 per piece
- Equipment wear, photography, packaging: $5-10 per piece
- **Total Overhead: $14-25**

**True Cost:**
- Materials ($14 avg) + Labor ($52 avg) + Overhead ($20 avg) = **$86**

**Wholesale Price:**
- $86 × 2 = **$172**

**Retail Price:**
- $172 × 2 = **$344**

**Your $100 Price = 58% of true retail value**

## Why $100 is Justified (and potentially underpriced)

### 1. Geological Glazes Add Real Value

**Raw material glazes are a premium differentiator:**

- **Wood ash glazes**: Each batch is unique based on wood source, combustion temperature, and application thickness. Creates depth, variation, and organic patterns commercial glazes cannot match.
- **Volcanic ash**: Adds genuine geological texture and micro-crystalline structures. Collectible materials with narrative value.
- **Iron oxide**: Creates depth, movement, and variation. Each piece is unique based on reduction/oxidation firing.

**Market premium for raw material glazes: +40-60%**

### 2. One-of-One Scarcity

Production ceramicists price for volume. You're pricing for uniqueness:

- **Production work**: 50-100 identical pieces = economies of scale
- **Your work**: 1 piece = collector value, scarcity premium
- **Market comparison**: Limited edition prints vs. originals pricing

**Scarcity premium: +30-50%**

### 3. LA/Long Beach Market Positioning

**Demographic advantages:**
- High disposable income coastal population
- Art-collecting culture (galleries, art walks)
- Appreciation for handmade/craftsmanship
- Competition with boutique pricing ($85-125 for similar work in LA area)

**Market premium: +15-25%**

### 4. Artistic Differentiation

**Your aesthetic (dark, sci-fi, experimental):**
- Appeals to specific collector demographic
- Different from " farmhouse cute" mainstream
- Creates brand loyalty and recognition
- Sells to collectors, not casual buyers

**Artistic premium: +20-30%**

## Shipping Cost Analysis

### Domestic Shipping (Small Ceramics)

**USPS Priority Mail:**
- 2-3 day delivery
- $8-12 depending on zone
- Includes tracking
- Best for pieces under 2 lbs

**UPS Ground:**
- 3-5 day delivery
- $10-15 depending on zone
- Better insurance options
- Best for multiple pieces

**Packaging costs:**
- Small box: $1.50-2.50
- Bubble wrap: $0.75-1.00
- Packing paper: $0.25-0.50
- Label: $0.10-0.15

**Total shipping cost: $12-18 per piece**

**Recommendation: Charge $15 shipping, or FREE shipping on orders $150+**

## Membership Studio vs. Own Kiln Impact

### Membership Studio Costs (LA Area)

**Monthly fees:**
- Basic access: $175-225/month
- Full access (firing included): $250-298/month
- Clay/glaze storage: +$25-50/month

**Impact on pricing:**
- Adds $9-15 per piece (at 20 pieces/month)
- No equipment depreciation costs
- No kiln maintenance/repair costs
- Flexible production schedule

### Own Kiln Comparison

**Upfront investment:**
- Kiln: $2,500-4,000
- Venting: $500-1,000
- Shelving/tools: $500-1,000
- **Total: $3,500-6,000**

**Ongoing costs:**
- Electricity: $8-15 per firing
- Maintenance: $200-500/year
- Depreciation: $300-500/year

**Break-even:** 3-5 years at current production volume

**Conclusion:** Membership studio is economically superior for your current scale. Revisit kiln ownership when producing 50+ pieces/month consistently.
