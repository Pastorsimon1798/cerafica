## 1. Ceruleix-2

**Photo:** `IMG_4908.JPG`
**Mood:** dramatic

### Caption

Planet Ceruleix-2. Woodsmoke, ancient fire

This is the first piece in a series of the new Glace Exploration Series I'm doing. It's inspired by planetary geology, by exoplanets, by the solar system, by everything that's going on in space exploration.  The idea is this: the surface of every vessel in this set (this is the first one, but this is going to be a series) is like the surface of a planet. We need to come up with a full story about how the visual features of the piece are actually correlated to the terrain and the geology of this exoplanet. The colors, the craters, and then also go deeper and include the actual glaze chemistry into the geologic composition of this fictional planet.  I also want to know a story about how the planet formed, what the features are made of, does anything live there? If anything does, what is that like? What are the plants like? What are the animals like? Are there people? Are there cities? Is it polluted? Is it beautiful? Is it a paradise? Is it a nightmare? Is it modern? Is it old technology?

The air here is woodsmoke, ancient fire.

Controlled-feeling — stable 18°C.

The dominant mineral here is earth tones.

Everything here reflects.

Light doubles back on itself until you cannot tell source from reflection.

Small cup or tumbler with layered cobalt-copper reduction glaze - glossy cratered surface with carbon trapping, exposed iron-rich clay foot visible.

This is a one-of-one world. Once claimed, it's gone. DM to inquire.
